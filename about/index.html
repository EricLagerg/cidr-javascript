Subnetting Calculator

What is it?

My subnet calculator will perform any computation on a submask, CIDR value, or IPv4 address. For example, it'll convert a submask to CIDR notation (e.g. 255.255.252.0 -> /22). Among other things, it'll also tell you the maximum number of hosts per network, the class of network, and number of subnets.

How do I use it?

One of two ways. The first would be to put in either your submask or CIDR number (e.g. 255.255.255.0 OR /24) and then your IP. This will fill out all fields correctly. If you just want to convert a submask to CIDR or visa versa, simply ignore the IP line. The second would be to enter the number of hosts you wish to serve (e.g. 500 or 250 or 65534) in addition to your IP. Again, if you don't have an IP and just want to see the submask/CIDR that your number of hosts requires, then ignore that field.

RememberIf you get "NaN" or "undefined" then either you've left the IP field blank OR you've messed something up. There's not a lot of error checking -- so as long as you don't try to do something weird like use the IP "523.532.124.999", you'll be fine. It's 100% accurate. I promise.

How do I use it on my website?

It's simple: include the JavaScript file "master.js" on your website, and make sure you have the form/table HTML files found here.
